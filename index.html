Vasil Ivanov Kunchev

The Apostle Levski

He was the ideologist and organizer of the Bulgarian national revolution. He traveled the country and established private revolutionary committees to prepare a general revolution. His dream was a pure and holy republic in which all would have equal rights, regardless of their nationality or religion.

Biography

Birth

18 July 1937

Vasil Ivanov Kunchev (as his birth name) was born right in center of the Balkan - Karlovo. Unfortunately he is left without a father from a young age and soon his uncle starts to take care of him.

The Deacon Ignatius

1852 - 1858

For a short time the hero that we know today would have almost been forgotten by history. Levski studied hard to become a priest but he knew that wasn't for him. So he left...

Levski the Flag bearer

1862 - 1868

On a late cold winter's day in the year of 1962 he left his home town to venture into the free world. He went straight to Belgrade (capital of Serbia) to enlist in the First Bulgarian Legion. His first and only duty there was as a Flag Bearer.

Internal Revolutionary organization

1871 - 1873

Levski has acoomplished so much during his life time but the greatest achievements of them all is the Internal Revolutionary organization. In the span of 3 years he travelled in the Ottoman Empire collecting donations from wealthy bulgarians, established reveolutionary regions all for the cause of the independence of the nation.

Death

18 February 1873

Not every hero survives to see what he has accomplished. But Vasil Levski layed the foundations for the revolution from Ottoman rule of 1876. Achievement with which not many are known for!

"If I win, I win for the whole nation - if I lose, I lose only myself."

Homage

Every year on 19th of february bulgarians from all over the country and abroad come to the monument of their hero in their respective hometown to pay tribute to their apostle. To be an Apostle is a title given to the people who fought for the freedom of our country with the power of speech and also with iron and blood.
